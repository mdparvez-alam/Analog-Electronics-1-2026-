# Calculations – Question 4

## Question 4(a)  
**Determine the thermal voltage for a diode at a temperature of 25°C.**

### Given:
- Temperature, T₍C₎ = 25°C  
- Boltzmann constant, k = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K  
- Charge of an electron, q = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C  

### Formula:
Thermal voltage is given by:

Vₜ = kT / q

Where  
T₍K₎ = T₍C₎ + 273

### Step-by-step calculation:
T₍K₎ = 25 + 273 = **298 K**

Vₜ = (1.38 × 10⁻²³ × 298) / (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)

Vₜ ≈ **0.0257 V**

### Final Answer:
**Thermal voltage Vₜ ≈ 25.7 mV**

## Question 4(b)  
**For the same diode, find the diode current using the diode equation.**

### Given:
- Iₛ = 40 nA = 40 × 10⁻⁹ A  
- n = 2  
- Vₜ = 0.0257 V  
- Vᴅ = 0.5 V  

### Formula:
Iᴅ = Iₛ (e^(Vᴅ / nVₜ) − 1)

### Calculation:
Vᴅ / nVₜ = 0.5 / (2 × 0.0257) ≈ 9.73

Iᴅ = 40 × 10⁻⁹ (e⁹·⁷³ − 1)

e⁹·⁷³ ≈ 1.68 × 10⁴

Iᴅ ≈ 40 × 10⁻⁹ × 1.68 × 10⁴

Iᴅ ≈ **0.00067 A**

### Final Answer:
**Diode current Iᴅ ≈ 0.67 mA**
